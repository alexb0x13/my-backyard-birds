Woodpeckers

Pipilo pipilo

When my family first moved to Indiana in 1972, red-headed woodpeckers (sometimes called “flag birds” because of their coloring) were almost common. My daughters and I lived for six weeks at the Surrey Inn in downtown Columbus (it’s now Bartholomew County offices) and on weekends we would explore the area. We were thrilled to see red-heads in some numbers at Brown County State Park. When we moved into our home in Taylorsville red-heads nested in old hickory trees and were frequent visitors to our feeders. I recall one Thanksgiving seeing four or five of them pecking corn from dry cobs beneath my kitchen window.

In the fall of 2011 a red-head visited my feeding station for the corn in cobs hanging for the squirrels. What a thrill to see this beautiful bird after so many years without it. He came for a few weeks and then not again until spring when a pair nested in the area. I did notice that while the red-head was “in residence” the red-bellied woodpecker was not.

I enjoy the yellow-shafted flickers, red-bellied woodpeckers, hairy and downies that frequent the area.
